1.0 ANOMALY DESCRIPTION

1.1 Physical Description

ARC-303 is a temporal predator with no stable physical form. It cannot be fully seen; instead, it manifests as brief distortions in perception, like a heat shimmer in the air or a fleeting shadow caught in peripheral vision. When captured by recording devices, both image and sound are distorted, as if a tape had been overwritten multiple times. Rarely, in reflective surfaces (mirrors, puddles, polished metals), it appears momentarily as a vaguely humanoid silhouette, but its proportions are entirely wrong: its limbs are unnaturally long and thin, and its joints are in the wrong places. Where a face should be, there is only a smooth, light-absorbing void.

1.2 Anomalous Effect & Behavior

ARC-303 hunts humans, but does not target their bodies or lives. Instead, it consumes their personal timelines—memories, identity, and historical existence. This process occurs in three stages:

Stage 1: Marking: Once it selects a victim, it stalks them for days or even weeks. During this time, the victim experiences small but increasingly severe temporal anomalies in daily life.

Stage 2: Feeding: When the victim is sufficiently isolated and psychologically weakened, ARC-303 strikes. This is not a physical attack. The victim becomes suddenly paralyzed and feels their entire life flashing before their eyes—backwards.

Stage 3: Hollow Echo: After feeding, the victim does not die. Physically, they are unharmed. But they are no longer themselves—only a biological shell remains.

1.3 Discovery Summary

A.R.C. assigned a field agent to investigate a surge of “unidentified individuals” in the REDACTED district of Istanbul. These cases matched no existing database entries. The agent’s final distorted transmission stated: “Command, listen… It’s here. In the reflection… in my reflection… I can see everything… it’s going backwards… my mother’s face… but… I’m forgetting… who am I… Command, I… I’m—” (Recording cuts off). The agent was found a day later as a “Hollow Echo.”

2.2 New Protocol: "Blind Eye Protocol"
